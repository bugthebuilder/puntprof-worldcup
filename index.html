Quantified Model Uncertainty
Football is a low-scoring, high-variance sport. In any given 90-minute match, the favourite wins approximately 45–55% of the time at best. Even with elite data inputs, group stage outcomes carry high uncertainty because:

1. Injury developments after this analysis (June 11 data) may change outcomes materially.
2. Tactical innovations — any manager can deploy a surprise system that disrupts expected patterns.
3. Referee decisions, set pieces, and penalties introduce significant non-quantifiable variance.
4. Psychological/motivational factors (host effect, penalty shootout history) are partially modelled but imprecise.
5. Weather and pitch conditions across 16 host cities vary significantly.
